Problem

Mining sites can absorb supplier underperformance for a while before it becomes visible in procurement reporting. Delays, partial deliveries, stockouts, and supplier-related incidents often appear as separate issues when they are really part of the same operating problem.

Context

The dataset models suppliers, purchase orders, deliveries, inventory status, incidents, and site summaries. The goal is to show how procurement and supplier data can be turned into operational supply reliability intelligence rather than a basic vendor scorecard.

Dataset

The run uses a fixed snapshot date and random state so the outputs stay reproducible. Strategic suppliers generally perform better, urgent orders move faster but cost more, and stockout pressure links back to late or weakly fulfilled deliveries without becoming perfectly correlated.

Insights

Supplier risk: Oreline Materials sits at the top of the current risk ranking. The issue is not price alone. It is the combination of delay, fulfilment quality, and operational exposure.
Inventory pressure: SITE_07 shows the strongest stockout pressure in Grinding Media, which points to a category-site control problem rather than a broad inventory issue.
Incident impact: Partial delivery disruption carries the largest combined downtime and cost impact in the current run, which makes supply reliability an operating KPI, not only a procurement KPI.

Recommendations

  • Escalate intervention on the highest-risk suppliers in critical categories before re-running sourcing decisions on cost alone.
  • Track urgent-order share by site and category as a planning pressure signal rather than a normal operating state.
  • Strengthen category-site controls where stockouts and delay exposure repeatedly overlap.
  • Bring downtime and incident cost into supplier governance so reliability is judged on operating impact, not just PO performance.
